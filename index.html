Introduction

As the Web transitions toward user-sovereign identity, data, and digital assets, ZKPs and blockchain provide the cryptographic substrate for “verify without reveal” and “coordinate without a central intermediary.” Simultaneously, the emergence of practical quantum computing pressures existing primitives, making post-quantum, decentralized infrastructure an urgent Web concern rather than a niche cryptography topic.

ZABAPAD positions itself squarely at this intersection: Web-native authentication and account abstraction with ZK, privacy-preserving data flows, Layer-2 proving and verification services, and quantum-resistant migration of browser/server-side protocols. The scope directly engages researchers and practitioners across security and privacy, decentralized systems, cryptography, and Web standards, providing a venue where methods, deployments, and policy/standardization needs can meet.

The Web Conference uniquely bridges algorithms, systems, security and privacy, economics, and policy within a single venue. ZABAPAD complements this breadth by concentrating on decentralized trust under post-quantum constraints and by emphasizing interoperability and standardization, ultimately preparing for the upcoming Web 4.0 era.

Call for Papers

We are seeking original, unpublished works related to zero-knowledge, blockchain, and quantum computing suitable for the post-quantum and decentralization era. The topic is not limited to a specific field, and all works are welcome.

Submission Site: OpenReview (TBD)

Format: Submissions must be in English, in double-column format, and must adhere to the ACM template and format (also available in Overleaf).

Extended Abstract: 2 pages in length, including references.

Short Paper: 4 pages in length, including references.

Full Paper: 8 pages as self-contained main paper, with additional pages for references and an optional Appendix, up to a maximum of 12 pages in total.
